# Executive Summary

<p className="pf-page-lead">
  This audit evaluated {config.name}'s provably fair architecture across <strong>eight core games</strong>: Dice, Crash, Plinko, Blackjack, Roulette, Keno, Mines, and Beef. The objective was to determine whether game outcomes are cryptographically reproducible, statistically sound, and <strong>resistant to structural manipulation</strong>.
</p>

Validation was conducted across **five domains**: deterministic outcome generation, entropy integrity, live-to-verifier parity, RTP mathematical accuracy, and structured fairness integrity testing. Assessment combined source-level review, independent deterministic recomputation, large-scale statistical simulation, and controlled adversarial testing.

<ArchDiagram config={config} />

Live production bets were captured and recomputed using disclosed inputs. Monte Carlo simulations validated theoretical RTP alignment across configurable parameters. Fairness integrity testing verified seed commitment, nonce sequencing, outcome determinism, round isolation, and payout integrity under adversarial conditions.

Across all reviewed games, outcomes were found to be **cryptographically reproducible**, **statistically unbiased** within expected variance tolerances, and **consistent with documented payout structures** at the time of audit.

All datasets, tooling, scripts, simulations, and verification logic referenced in this report are publicly accessible within the respective game sections. Any third party can **independently recompute and verify** the exact results presented in this audit, without relying on trust in ProvablyFair.org or the operator.

<p className="pf-muted">Certification reflects the integrity of the audited implementation as deployed during the review period.</p>

<SectionHeading id="entropy-architecture" label="Randomness" title="Entropy Architecture" />

Duel uses **HMAC-SHA256** as its deterministic RNG across seed-based games including Dice, Plinko, Blackjack, Keno, and Mines.

For Crash and Castle Roulette, Duel integrates **drand** — a decentralized public randomness beacon — as an additional entropy anchor. In these games:

<Checklist>
  <li>The server seed is combined with drand beacon output</li>
  <li>The drand round number is publicly verifiable</li>
  <li>Beacon output cannot be influenced retroactively</li>
</Checklist>

This architecture ensures outcome entropy is deterministic, reproducible, cryptographically verifiable, and externally anchored where applicable.

<Note variant="ok"><strong>No hidden entropy sources identified.</strong> Outcome entropy is deterministic, reproducible, cryptographically verifiable, and externally anchored where applicable.</Note>
